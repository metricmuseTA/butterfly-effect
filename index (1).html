Chapter 01
Runtime & Pacing — Did Films Actually Get Shorter?
Runtime Analysis · Muhammad Monis

Films Got Longer.
Not Shorter.

Conventional wisdom says short-form content compressed audience attention spans and forced Hollywood to make shorter films. The data says otherwise — adult dramas got longer, not shorter, in the Short-Form Era.

Average Runtime by Genre — Pre Short-Form Era (2005–2014)
Average Runtime by Genre — Short-Form Era (2015–2025)
+9 min
Adult drama runtime increase — 125 → 134 minutes in Short-Form Era
100%
Musical reviews mention runtime in Short-Form Era vs. 0% pre-era
50%
Sci-Fi reviews mention time in Pre-Era — the genre already had patience expectations
Key Finding 1

Films are not getting shorter in response to short-form content — adult-oriented genres like drama are actually running longer, suggesting filmmakers are doubling down on depth rather than shrinking to compete.

Key Finding 2

Musicals emerged as the most time-sensitive genre in the Short-Form Era, with 100% of reviews in that period referencing runtime — a dramatic shift from zero mentions pre-2015.

Key Finding 3

The "time bucket" distribution reveals audiences are spending more time with films than the short-form narrative suggests — the data contradicts the compression hypothesis.

Time Bucket % by Genre — Pre vs Short-Form Era
"The assumption was that TikTok killed our patience for long films. But adults are going to the cinema to escape the scroll — and they're staying longer than ever."
— Chapter 1 Finding, The Butterfly Effect
Chapter 02
Hype vs. Reality — The Expectation Gap
Hype Gap Analysis · Tiffani Anderson

The Gap Is Closing.

Before 2015, audience expectations routinely outpaced experience — films were overhyped and underdelivered. In the Short-Form Era, that gap has narrowed by 54%, and for the first time, zero pre-era films were over-hyped.

Hype Gap by Era (Avg. IMDb Score − Avg. Audience Score)
Hype vs. Reaction Scatter — Both Eras
−22.84
Average hype gap pre-2015 — audiences consistently underwhelmed vs. critic expectations
−10.49
Average hype gap post-2015 — a 54% recovery toward alignment
+12.35
Points of hype gap recovered — the measurable butterfly effect on expectation calibration
Key Finding 1

Zero films in the Pre Short-Form Era (2005–2014) were over-hyped — every single film underperformed audience expectations relative to critic scores, suggesting a systematic inflation in critical reception.

Key Finding 2

The Short-Form Era narrowed the hype gap from -22.84 to -10.49 — a 54% improvement, suggesting that social media exposure is calibrating audience expectations before they enter the theater.

Key Finding 3

The narrowing gap suggests audiences in the Short-Form Era are better informed — viral clips, trailer reactions, and creator commentary on platforms like TikTok give audiences accurate signal before opening weekend.

Hype Gap Distribution — Before and After 2015
"Short-form didn't just change how we watch films. It changed how prepared we are before we sit down. The hype gap closing isn't a coincidence — it's an information effect."
— Chapter 2 Finding, The Butterfly Effect
Chapter 03
The Language Shift — How Audiences Talk About Cinema
NLP Analysis · Muhammad Monis

The Words Changed.

Natural language processing of 81 review data points across both eras reveals a measurable shift in how audiences articulate their film experience — more impatience, more engagement language, and a persistent focus on craft.

Keyword Bucket % Over Time (2005–2025)
NLP Shift by Category — Pre vs Short-Form Era
+17.8%
Time friction language — "too long," "dragged," "pacing" mentions increased
+29.4%
Engagement language — "binge," "hooked," "couldn't look away" surged in Short-Form Era
+6.5%
Craft critique — technical language about cinematography, score, and performance increased
Top Keywords — Pre Short-Form Era (2005–2014)
Top Keywords — Short-Form Era (2015–2025)
Key Finding 1

Time friction language (+17.8%) confirms audiences trained on short-form content are more aware of — and vocal about — pacing issues, even as they continue watching longer films.

Key Finding 2

Engagement language surged +29.4%, suggesting short-form audiences bring a binge-consumption frame to cinema: they want to be gripped, and they have the vocabulary to say when they are.

Key Finding 3

Craft critique increased +6.5% — a counterintuitive finding suggesting short-form content may have created more visually literate viewers, not less attentive ones.

Key Finding 4

The keyword shift across all three categories reinforces a single thesis: short-form content didn't diminish cinema — it changed the contract between filmmakers and audiences.

"The language shift doesn't tell us audiences are less patient — it tells us they're more articulate. Short-form gave them a vocabulary for what they want from cinema."
— Chapter 3 Finding, The Butterfly Effect
Methodology
Full-Stack Data Pipeline — Bronze / Silver / Gold Architecture
Technical Approach

How We Built It

A complete medallion architecture pipeline built on Databricks, analyzing 50 films across two defined eras using three independent analytical workstreams.

01 · Bronze
Data Ingestion
Raw ingest from IMDb, Rotten Tomatoes, and audience review datasets. 50 films selected across Pre Short-Form (2005–2014) and Short-Form (2015–2025) eras. Raw tables stored in Databricks Unity Catalog.
02 · Silver
Cleaning & Normalization
Deduplication, null handling, score normalization, and era classification. Runtime data cleaned to minutes. Review text tokenized and preprocessed. Hype gap calculated as critic score minus audience score.
03 · Gold — Runtime
Runtime & Pacing Analysis
Genre-level runtime aggregation across both eras. Time bucket distribution analysis. Power BI visual built by Muhammad Monis revealing the counterintuitive lengthening effect.
04 · Gold — Hype
Hype Gap Analysis
hype_gap_table: 50 rows, one per film. Calculated per-film and era-level hype gap. Scatter analysis revealing zero over-hyped films pre-2015 and a 54% gap recovery in Short-Form Era.
05 · Gold — NLP
Language Shift Analysis
nlp_language_shift: 81 rows. Keyword bucket classification across three categories (time friction, engagement, craft). Trend analysis over 20 years revealing directional shifts in audience vocabulary.
06 · Visualization
Multi-Platform Dashboards
Tiffani: Databricks SQL + Tableau. Monis: Power BI with NLP visual. Project website: fully self-contained HTML/JS. All visuals reference the same Gold layer tables.
